1. Beer Oktoberfes Festival

Germany cancels world's largest beer festival
The world's largest beer festival, Oktoberfest, has been canceled for the second year in a row because of Covid-19. German officials said they would cancel this year's Oktoberfest, due to the crisis from the Covid-19 pandemic. The decision was made by the head of Bavaria, Markus Söder, and the mayor of Munich, Dieter Reiter. Previously, Oktoberfest was scheduled to be held from September 18 to October 3. This is a global festival, but the pandemic has not been brought under control enough for people from all over the world to gather in Bavaria, Mr. Söder said. "It is not possible to practice social distancing and wear masks in the festival beer tents. The situation is too precarious. Imagine there is a new wave of disease and the event will become a super-contagion. The festival's brand will be permanently affected and we don't want that to happen," he said.

Rejoice in the beer-drinking holiday --- Source: Google.com

Mayor Reiter said the Oktoberfest cancellation was "a great regret" for millions of fans. He also warned that the cancellation of the festival for two consecutive years would have direct impacts on local workers, from hotel and restaurant owners to service workers. It is estimated that in 2019, the beer festival brought in $1.5 billion to the local economy. Historically, this festival has been canceled several times. It was not held during the years of World War II (1946-1948) and 1854, when there was a cholera outbreak in Munich. The organizers expect the festival in 2022 to really explode because fans are eager to join the party again.

2. Brazilian Carnival

Brazil cancels carnival for the first time in more than 100 years
The famous Brazilian Carnival was canceled because of the resurgence of Covid-19 and the number of deaths in this country was the second highest in the world.

The masquerade mask --- Source: Baoquocte.vn

Carnival was scheduled to take place on February 12-17, 2021, but Brazil was fighting a second wave of Covid-19 outbreaks, so the festival was cancelled. Carnival in Rio de Janeiro (Brazil) is held annually in February - March attracting about 2 million people every day all over the streets. The first Carnival was held in 1723 in Rio de Janeiro. The festival includes a variety of street performances that attract artists, samba schools and visitors from around the world. This is an opportunity for visitors to immerse themselves in seductive samba dances, music-filled streets and hot parties. The focal point of the Brazilian Carnival is the Sambadrome square with colorful costume parades.

3. Chinese Lantern Festival.

The Chinese Forbidden City is resplendent with lanterns --- Source: Vnexpress.net

The Forbidden City opens at night for the first time in 94 years
The famous building in Beijing (China) will be open to visitors on the evening of February 19 and 20. This is a rare opportunity for anyone who wishes to see the lanterns lit up, in the historical setting of the Forbidden City. The event is part of Beijing's lantern festival, which takes place on the night of February 19 and 20, marking the end of the Lunar New Year holiday, according to CNN.

The Forbidden City will officially open the garden of King Qianlong in 2020 --- Source: Vnexpress.net

The atmosphere of the festival is very bustling and vibrant, cheerful and happy smiles are always on everyone's lips. Not only that, activities such as hanging wishes, solving puzzles imbued with traditional culture also take place to attract people and visitors to experience. And the main highlight in this festival is releasing lanterns, this is an activity that everyone is looking forward to. The person who releases the lantern will write his or her New Year's wishes on the lantern and release them into the sky. People believe that paper lanterns will bring their wishes to God. The lights are released into the sky as well as the wishes and dreams of the people here fly high and always shine.

The project is preserved by the World Heritage Foundation and the Palace Museum of China --- Source : Vnexpress.net

4. Voodoo Festival, Benin

The Voodoo creed is considered one of the oldest festivals in the world. Today, this belief still exists and is on the list of the most unique and bizarre festivals in the world. In the Ouidah region of Benin - an African country considered a holy place for the gods, Voodoo practitioners, like shamans, will take over the worship of the gods.

People dancing in voodoo festival Nguồn: Pinterest.com

Voodoo is highly regarded in Benin. Every year, the Voodoo festival attracts crowds of curious people with dozens of unique rituals. Animal sacrifice is considered by the people here as a good thing with the idea that if you sacrifice something to the gods, they will bless you and give you health. This sacrifice appears in many myths in both Nigeria, Togo, Ghana, neighboring countries of Benin.
